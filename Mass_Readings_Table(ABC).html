Christmas & Epiphany Season
New Year's Day
Mary, Mother of God Sol.		 No No — fixed No Num 6:22–27 · Ps 67 · Gal 4:4–7 · Luke 2:16–21 — Same readings every year. Jan 1 = Octave of Christmas.
Epiphany Sol. No No — fixed No Isa 60:1–6 · Ps 72 · Eph 3:2–3a, 5–6 · Matt 2:1–12 — Same readings Years A/B/C. US: celebrated on Sunday between Jan 2–8.
Ordinary Time — Feasts of the Lord
Presentation of the Lord
(Blessing of Candles — Candlemas) Feast		 No No — fixed Yes — 2nd reading Mal 3:1–4 · Ps 24 · Heb 2:14–18 · Luke 2:22–40 Alt 2nd reading: Heb 2:14–18 or Heb 2:14b–17
Long or short form of Gospel also permitted

Parish / Pastoral Masses (readings chosen by celebrant)
CNY Mass Parish N/A N/A Many options Weekday readings of the day, or Masses for Various Needs & Occasions (Vol. IV Lectionary) — e.g. "For the Nation" or "For a Happy Death" or Votive Mass of BVM Not a universal feast. Celebrant selects from Ritual/Votive Masses or weekday readings.
Mass for Anointing of the Sick Ritual N/A N/A Many options Ritual Mass: "For the Sick" — multiple options provided in Lectionary Vol. IV E.g. 1st reading from Jas 5:13–16; Ps 102 or 27; Gospel from Matt 8 or Mark 6 (among others). Celebrant selects. Proper Ritual Mass with broad reading choices. No single fixed set.
Lent & Holy Week
Ash Wednesday Sol. No No — fixed No Joel 2:12–18 · Ps 51 · 2 Cor 5:20–6:2 · Matt 6:1–6, 16–18 — Same every year. Two readings before Gospel (rare for a weekday).
Rite of Send-Off (Sending Out of Catechumens) Rite N/A N/A Many options Readings of the Sunday on which it falls RCIA rite occurs within regular Sunday Mass; no separate lectionary set. Readings are those of the Sunday. Rite of Christian Initiation; takes place during Sunday Mass. Not a separate feast.
RCIA 1st Scrutiny Rite
(3rd Sunday of Lent)		 No Yes — A/B/C differ Yes — Year A preferred Year A (preferred for scrutinies): Exod 17:3–7 · Ps 95 · Rom 5:1–2, 5–8 · John 4:5–42 (Samaritan Woman) Year B alt: Exod 20:1–17 · Ps 19 · 1 Cor 1:22–25 · John 2:13–25
Year C alt: Exod 3:1–8a, 13–15 · Ps 103 · 1 Cor 10:1–6, 10–12 · Luke 13:1–9
Year A readings may be used in any year when scrutinies are celebrated.		 Lectionary explicitly allows Year A readings in B & C when scrutinies are celebrated.
RCIA 2nd Scrutiny Rite
(4th Sunday of Lent)		 No Yes — A/B/C differ Yes — Year A preferred Year A (preferred): 1 Sam 16:1b, 6–7, 10–13a · Ps 23 · Eph 5:8–14 · John 9:1–41 (Man Born Blind) Year B alt: 2 Chr 36:14–16, 19–23 · Ps 137 · Eph 2:4–10 · John 3:14–21
Year C alt: Josh 5:9a, 10–12 · Ps 34 · 2 Cor 5:17–21 · Luke 15:1–3, 11–32
Year A readings may replace B/C when scrutinies are celebrated.		 "Laetare Sunday" — rose vestments optional. Year A readings strongly recommended for parishes with elect.
RCIA 3rd Scrutiny Rite
(5th Sunday of Lent)		 No Yes — A/B/C differ Yes — Year A preferred Year A (preferred): Ezek 37:12–14 · Ps 130 · Rom 8:8–11 · John 11:1–45 (Raising of Lazarus) Year B alt: Jer 31:31–34 · Ps 51 · Heb 5:7–9 · John 12:20–33
Year C alt: Isa 43:16–21 · Ps 126 · Phil 3:8–14 · John 8:1–11
Year A preferred when elect are present.		 Third and final scrutiny before Easter. Year A readings are normative for RCIA parishes.
Annunciation Sol. No No — fixed No Isa 7:10–14; 8:10 · Ps 40 · Heb 10:4–10 · Luke 1:26–38 — Mar 25. Fixed readings. If it falls in Holy Week, transferred to after Easter Octave.
Lenten Penitential Service Parish N/A N/A Many options Not a Mass; Liturgy of the Word only Penitential liturgies draw from Rite of Penance Appendix II. Suggested texts include Isa 53; Ps 51; Luke 15; John 8, etc. Priest/deacon selects. Communal Penance Service — not a Mass. No single prescribed set of readings.
Palm Sunday
(Passion Sunday) Sol.		 No Yes — Gospel changes A/B/C Yes — Procession Gospel Procession Gospel (changes each year):
A: Matt 21:1–11 · B: Mark 11:1–10 or John 12:12–16 · C: Luke 19:28–40
Fixed: Isa 50:4–7 · Ps 22 · Phil 2:6–11
Passion Gospel: A: Matt 26–27 · B: Mark 14–15 · C: Luke 22–23		 Passion Gospel: long or short form available each year.
Procession: in Year B, either Mark or John may be used.		 Two distinct Gospel moments: Procession & Passion. First reading and 2nd reading are fixed; Passion Gospel changes yearly.
Easter Triduum
Maundy Thursday
(Mass of the Lord's Supper) Sol.		 No No — fixed No Exod 12:1–8, 11–14 · Ps 116 · 1 Cor 11:23–26 · John 13:1–15 — Evening Mass only (not morning). Begins the Triduum. Fixed readings every year.
Good Friday
(Liturgy of the Lord's Passion) Sol.		 No No — fixed Yes — Passion long/short form Isa 52:13–53:12 · Ps 31 · Heb 4:14–16; 5:7–9 · John 18:1–19:42 Passion (John 18–19): long form or short form (John 18:1–19:30 only) permitted. Not a Mass — Liturgy of the Word + Veneration of the Cross + Communion. No Eucharistic Prayer. Fixed readings.
Easter Vigil Sol. No (is itself the vigil) Yes — Gospel changes A/B/C Yes — OT readings (min. 3 of 7) 7 OT readings (at least 3 required, all 7 recommended):
Gen 1; Gen 22; Exod 14; Isa 54; Isa 55; Bar 3; Ezek 36
NT: Rom 6:3–11 · Ps 118 · Gospel: A: Matt 28:1–10 · B: Mark 16:1–7 · C: Luke 24:1–12		 Parishes may choose which OT readings to include (minimum 3, always including Exod 14). Responsorial psalms are prescribed for each OT reading used. Most readings of any liturgy in the year. OT readings are flexible within prescribed set. Gospel varies by year.

Pentecost Sol. Yes — extended vigil Yes — 2nd reading & Gospel in B & C Yes — Vigil: 4 OT options Vigil (any one of 4 OT readings): Gen 11:1–9 or Exod 19:3–8, 16–20 or Ezek 37:1–14 or Joel 3:1–5
Day Mass: Acts 2:1–11 · Ps 104 · 1 Cor 12:3b–7, 12–13 (Yr A) · John 20:19–23 (Yr A)		 Year B: 2nd reading Gal 5:16–25 (alt.); Gospel John 15:26–27; 16:12–15 (alt.)

Sequence Veni Sancte Spiritus is obligatory.		 Richest set of alternatives of any feast. Vigil has 4 interchangeable OT readings; Day Mass has year-specific alternatives in B & C.

Transfiguration of the Lord
("Feast Day") Feast		 No Yes — Gospel changes A/B/C No Dan 7:9–10, 13–14 · Ps 97 · 2 Pet 1:16–19
Gospel: A: Matt 17:1–9 · B: Mark 9:2–10 · C: Luke 9:28b–36		 — Aug 6. Listed as "Feast Day" in your calendar. First two readings fixed every year; Gospel varies by A/B/C.
Assumption Sunset / Vigil Sol. Yes — proper vigil No — vigil fixed No Vigil: 1 Chr 15:3–4, 15–16; 16:1–2 · Ps 132 · 1 Cor 15:54b–57 · Luke 11:27–28 — Aug 14 evening. Proper vigil with its own distinct readings (different from Day Mass).
Assumption (Day Mass) Sol. Yes No — fixed No Rev 11:19a; 12:1–6a, 10ab · Ps 45 · 1 Cor 15:20–27 · Luke 1:39–56 — Aug 15. Fixed readings every year. Holy Day of Obligation.
Mission Sunday Parish N/A N/A Many options Readings of the Sunday (World Mission Sunday = second-last Sunday of October) Celebrant may supplement with readings from Masses for Various Needs: "For the Spread of the Gospel" or "For Missionaries." Sunday readings take precedence. World Mission Sunday is the second-last Sunday of October (e.g. Oct 18, 2026). The Sunday readings of the liturgical year apply; no separate lectionary set.
November — Solemnities of All Saints & All Souls
All Saints Sunset / Vigil Sol. Yes No — fixed No No distinct vigil readings are prescribed in the standard Lectionary for All Saints. The evening Mass of Nov 1 uses the Day Mass readings. — Nov 1 evening. Unlike Assumption or Ascension, All Saints has no separate vigil Mass set; Day Mass readings are used throughout.
All Saints (Day) Sol. No No — fixed No Rev 7:2–4, 9–14 · Ps 24 · 1 John 3:1–3 · Matt 5:1–12a — Nov 1. Same readings every year. Holy Day of Obligation.
All Souls Sol. No No fixed cycle Many options — all readings From Masses for the Dead (Lectionary #668 onward). Many readings provided for each position. Examples: Wis 3:1–9 or Job 19; Ps 23 or Ps 27 or Ps 130; Rom 5:5–11 or Rom 6:3–9 or 1 Cor 15; John 5:25–29 or John 6:37–40 or John 11:17–27, etc. All Souls is the only Mass in the year where options exist for every reading. Priests may celebrate up to three Masses, each with different readings. Nov 2. Broadest reading flexibility of any day in the liturgical year. Lectionary gives extensive options for all three readings.
Advent & Christmas
Advent Penitential Service Parish N/A N/A Many options Not a Mass — Liturgy of the Word for communal penance Rite of Penance Appendix II suggests texts such as Isa 1 or Isa 55; Ps 51 or Ps 130; Rom 13; Luke 3 or Luke 15, etc. Priest selects. Same structure as Lenten Penitential Service. No prescribed reading set; celebrant chooses.
Christmas Midnight Mass Sol. No (is its own Mass) No — fixed No Isa 9:1–6 · Ps 96 · Titus 2:11–14 · Luke 2:1–14 — "Mass during the Night." Fixed readings every year A/B/C.
Christmas Evening / Sunset
(Vigil Mass) Sol.		 Yes — is the Vigil No — fixed Yes — short Gospel form Isa 62:1–5 · Ps 89:4–5, 16–17, 27, 29 · Acts 13:16–17, 22–25 · Matt 1:1–25 Gospel: long form Matt 1:1–25 or short form Matt 1:18–25. Vigil readings may also be used at any Christmas Day Mass per pastoral need. Dec 24 evening. The Vigil Mass has its own proper set of readings, distinct from the three Masses of Christmas Day. Fixed every year.
Christmas (Day Mass) Sol. Yes — Vigil Mass No — fixed Yes — 4 distinct Mass sets Mass during the Day: Isa 52:7–10 · Ps 98 · Heb 1:1–6 · John 1:1–18
Gospel: long form John 1:1–18 or short form John 1:1–5, 9–14
 4 Mass sets, all fixed (no A/B/C variation):
1) Vigil: Isa 62:1–5 · Acts 13 · Matt 1:1–25 (or 1:18–25)
2) Midnight: Isa 9 · Titus 2 · Luke 2:1–14
3) Dawn: Isa 62:11–12 · Titus 3 · Luke 2:15–20
4) Day: Isa 52 · Heb 1 · John 1:1–18 or 1:1–5, 9–14		 Dec 25. One of only four feasts with four distinct Mass texts. Each Mass has fixed readings; no year-A/B/C variation.
Sacramental / Special Occasion Masses
Confirmation Mass Ritual N/A N/A Many options Ritual Mass "For the Conferral of Confirmation" — Lectionary Vol. IV. If on a Sunday outside Lent/Easter, Sunday readings take precedence but Confirmation proper readings may be used on weekdays. Suggested readings include: Acts 8:14–17 or Acts 19:1–6; Ps 104; Rom 8:14–17 or Eph 1:3–7; John 14:15–17 or John 16:12–15 (among others). Bishop/priest selects. Sept 5, 4pm (your calendar). Ritual Mass with broad options. If it falls on a Sunday, the Bishop may ask to use the Ritual Mass readings as an exception.
